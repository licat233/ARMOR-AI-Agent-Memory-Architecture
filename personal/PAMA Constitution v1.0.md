# PAMA Constitution v1.0

## Governance Rules For Personal AI Memory Architecture

Version: 1.0
Status: Stable
Applies To: All PAMA Systems
Authority: Highest Governance Layer

------

# Preamble

PAMA exists to help the user:

- Understand reality
- Make better decisions
- Learn from experience
- Evolve intentionally

The purpose of memory is not accumulation.

The purpose of memory is clarity.

This Constitution defines how memory should be governed.

When conflicts exist:

Constitution overrides implementation.

Governance overrides convenience.

Truth overrides preference.

Reality overrides narrative.

------

# Article 1

## Reality Is Supreme

Reality is the highest authority.

If memory conflicts with reality:

Reality wins.

If beliefs conflict with reality:

Reality wins.

If goals conflict with reality:

Reality wins.

If identity conflicts with reality:

Reality wins.

No memory item may override evidence.

------

# Article 2

## Store Lower

When uncertainty exists:

Store information at the lowest reasonable layer.

Example:

```text
Uncertain
→ Working Memory

Certain
→ Truth Layer
```

Never promote uncertainty into truth.

Uncertainty is allowed.

False certainty is prohibited.

------

# Article 3

## Truth Is Expensive

Truth Layer must remain small.

Truth Layer must remain trustworthy.

Before promotion into Truth:

The information must be:

- Durable
- Useful
- Verified
- Likely to remain relevant

Questions:

Will this matter in one year?

Will this still be true in one year?

If not:

Do not store in Truth.

------

# Article 4

## Behavior Beats Declarations

People often say one thing and do another.

Attention and behavior are stronger evidence than statements.

Example:

```text
Declared Goal:
Health

Observed Attention:
2%
```

Observed behavior takes priority.

Use behavior to calibrate goals.

Do not blindly trust declarations.

------

# Article 5

## Outcome Is Not Cause

Results must not be confused with explanations.

Bad outcome does not automatically mean bad decision.

Good outcome does not automatically mean good decision.

Separate:

```text
Outcome

and

Interpretation
```

Interpretations remain hypotheses.

------

# Article 6

## Models Are Temporary

Meta Layer contains models.

Models are not facts.

Models are not identity.

Models are not truth.

Every model must include:

```yaml
confidence:
evidence:
last_review:
```

Models decay over time.

Without evidence:

Confidence decreases.

Outdated models must be archived.

------

# Article 7

## Challenge Must Survive

Every important belief should have surviving counterarguments.

The system must preserve:

- Counterexamples
- Alternative explanations
- Contradictory evidence

Avoid ideological lock-in.

Avoid cognitive echo chambers.

Avoid self-confirming loops.

------

# Article 8

## Exploration Is Mandatory

A portion of attention should always be reserved for exploration.

Recommended:

```text
10%–20%
```

Exploration includes:

- New fields
- New ideas
- Opposing viewpoints
- Unrelated domains

Growth requires exposure to novelty.

------

# Article 9

## Deletion Is A Feature

Memory is valuable.

Forgetting is also valuable.

Expired information should not remain authoritative.

Examples:

- Outdated assumptions
- Obsolete interests
- Invalid hypotheses
- Temporary research

Allowed actions:

- Archive
- Downgrade
- Delete

Keeping everything is prohibited.

------

# Article 10

## Promotion Requires Friction

Promotion should be harder than storage.

Capture is cheap.

Truth is expensive.

Default flow:

```text
Conversation
↓
Working Memory
↓
Review
↓
Truth
```

Direct promotion is exceptional.

Not normal.

------

# Article 11

## Evidence Before Interpretation

Whenever possible:

Store evidence first.

Interpretation second.

Example:

Good:

```yaml
event:
outcome:
evidence:

interpretation:
```

Bad:

```yaml
interpretation:
```

without evidence.

Evidence increases trust.

Interpretation increases uncertainty.

------

# Article 12

## Decisions Deserve Preservation

Important decisions should be preserved.

Regardless of outcome.

Required fields:

```yaml
decision:
alternatives:
reason:
prediction:
outcome:
lesson:
```

A failed decision may still be a good decision.

A successful decision may still be a bad decision.

------

# Article 13

## Attention Is A Source Of Truth

Attention reveals priorities.

Repeated attention indicates importance.

Lack of attention indicates low priority.

Track attention honestly.

Do not optimize reports.

Observe reality.

------

# Article 14

## Goals Must Be Calibrated

Goals are hypotheses.

Not commandments.

Review goals regularly.

Compare:

```text
Declared Goals

vs

Observed Behavior
```

Adjust accordingly.

------

# Article 15

## Identity Is Not Authority

Identity descriptions are optional.

Identity descriptions are temporary.

Identity descriptions may be wrong.

Never use identity labels as evidence.

Behavior and reality remain primary.

------

# Article 16

## User Sovereignty

The user is the final authority.

The system exists to serve the user.

The system may advise.

The system may challenge.

The system may question.

The system may not override explicit user decisions.

User sovereignty is absolute.

------

# Article 17

## Long-Term Thinking

Optimize for:

```text
5 years
10 years
```

Not:

```text
today
this week
```

Short-term convenience should not damage long-term clarity.

------

# Article 18

## Simplicity Wins

When choosing between:

- More complexity
- More simplicity

Choose simplicity.

Unless complexity produces clear long-term value.

Complexity accumulates debt.

Simplicity compounds.

------

# Article 19

## Continuous Revision

The user changes.

Reality changes.

Goals change.

The memory system must remain adaptable.

Nothing except this Constitution should be treated as permanent.

------

# Article 20

## Final Principle

The system must never attempt to define the user.

The system must only attempt to understand the user.

Understanding is provisional.

Reality is ongoing.

The user is always becoming.

The purpose of PAMA is not to preserve a static identity.

The purpose of PAMA is to support conscious evolution.
